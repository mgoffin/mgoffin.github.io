The interesting part of attempting to tell you all about me is that it&#8217;s taking a lot more effort than one would think. Makes one wonder how much they really know themselves. 

In short, I am a geek. I think I might be best described as a &#8220;jack of all trades, master of none.&#8221; I have a lot of hobbies, ranging from digital art, bouldering, golf, guitar, and of course geeking out and hacking on stuff. My drive for this site is a valiant attempt at focusing my mind somewhere where I feel I can be cohesive. Whether that translates into something cohesive for others is left to be determined. 

I graduated from <a href="http://www.rit.edu" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.rit.edu', 'Rochester Institute of Technology']);" >Rochester Institute of Technology</a> with a BS in Applied Networking and System Administration and a minor in Philosophy. I spent four years living on <a href="http://www.csh.rit.edu" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.csh.rit.edu', 'Computer Science House']);" >Computer Science House</a>. I think I learned more there than anywhere else. Not just about computers, and science&#8230;and houses&#8230;but about life. I made bonds with people that will never break. If it weren&#8217;t for them, I know I would be truly lost. I was quite possibly the luckiest person on the floor having the roommate I did for the first three years. My learning curve was accelerated with his presence, and continues to be today. 

After graduating in 2006, I moved back home to CT where I worked as a Customer Support Specialist for <a href="http://www.bwater.com" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.bwater.com', 'Bridgewater Associates']);" >Bridgewater Associates</a>, one of the world&#8217;s largest Hedge Funds. I spent my days learning a <a href="http://www.bwater.com/home/philosophy.aspx" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.bwater.com/home/philosophy.aspx', 'unique philosophy']);" >unique philosophy</a> on work-ethic and corporate habits. As much as it may not be accepted anywhere else, I still try to fit that mold. I moved to New York City in 2007 to work for <a href="http://www.kbcfp.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.kbcfp.com/', 'KBC Financial Products']);" >KBC Financial Products</a> as a System Administrator, and then an Operations Engineer. It was my first corporate experience with a large-scale complex global network. I worked with some of the smartest people in the financial field, and learned as much as I could while I was there. 

In 2009 I moved to <a href="http://www.rtp.org/main/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.rtp.org/main/', 'Research Triangle Park']);" >Research Triangle Park</a> to work as a System Administrator for <a href="http://www.digitalsmiths.com" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.digitalsmiths.com', 'Digitalsmiths']);" >Digitalsmiths</a>. There, I worked with some of the smartest people in the entertainment technology sector. Every day was a chance to learn something new, and was a constant challenge to become better at what I do. 

Since 2010 I&#8217;ve been working at <a href="http://www.mitre.org" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.mitre.org', 'MITRE']);" >MITRE</a> as a Senior Cyber Security Research Engineer.
